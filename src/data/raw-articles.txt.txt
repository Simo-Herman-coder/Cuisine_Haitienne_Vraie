ARTICLE 1: RANKING AND CULTURAL PRIDE
Title: Haïti: 57e meilleure cuisine du monde grâce à un restaurant de Milot!
Source: lescientifique.org / Taste Atlas Ranking
Content:
Taste Atlas, a platform dedicated to preserving local ingredients, traditional recipes, and authentic restaurants, ranked Haitian cuisine as the 57th best in the world in 2022 with a score of 3.93 out of 5. This monumental achievement is heavily credited to 'Lakou Lakay', a renowned traditional restaurant situated in Milot in the Northern Department of Haiti. Run by Maurice Étienne, Marie Innocente Conzé, and Rose Manette Beaulieu, Lakou Lakay is highly recommended to international tourists. Food is a diplomatic tool that showcases Haiti's rich cultural history, far beyond standard geopolitical clichés.

ARTICLE 2: ESSENTIAL RECIPES & TRADITIONS (BY EXPERT CHEFS)

[RECIPE 1] GRIOT (TRADITIONAL PORK DISH)
- History: The signature national dish of Haiti. Pork was historically rare and expensive, reserving Griot for special, festive celebrations.
- Ingredients: 2 lb pork shoulder (cubed 4cm), 2 lemons/limes, 1 green pepper, 1 bundle bouquet garni (parsley, thyme, bay leaf, rosemary), 1 onion (thickly sliced), 2 garlic cloves (minced), 1 Scotch bonnet pepper with 3 cloves poked into it, 1 tsp chicken broth powder, salt, pepper, oil for frying.
- Instructions: Rub meat with lemon/lime juice, rinse. Combine meat, citrus juices, and all aromatics in a pot. Marinate in the fridge for 2 hours. Add water to cover, simmer over medium heat for 20-30 minutes until tender. Strain meat (reserve liquid for sauce). Place meat on a sheet and bake/fry at 400°F (180°C) for 25 minutes until deep golden brown and crispy. Serve with pikliz.

[RECIPE 2] PIKLIZ (HAITIAN SPICY RELISH)
- History: An essential spicy condiment served alongside fried meats (griot, tassot) and bananes pesées to cut through the richness.
- Ingredients: 300g cabbage (finely shredded), 2 large carrots (grated/julienned), 1 red pepper (sliced), 2 onions, 1 shallot, 1-2 Scotch bonnet peppers (minced), white vinegar to cover, salt, pepper.
- Instructions: Ensure all shredded vegetables are dry. Toss cabbage, carrots, peppers, and onions together in a bowl with generous salt and pepper. Transfer tight into sterilized glass jars. Pour white vinegar to completely cover the vegetables. Seal hermetically and let rest in the fridge for 24 to 48 hours to mature.

[RECIPE 3] SOUP JOUMOU (INDEPENDENCE PUMPKIN SOUPE)
- History: A powerful symbol of freedom. Inscribed on UNESCO's Intangible Cultural Heritage list in 2021. Under French colonial rule, enslaved people prepared this savory soup but were forbidden from eating it. On January 1, 1804, revolutionary leader Jean-Jacques Dessalines declared independence, making Soup Joumou the national dish consumed every New Year's Day to celebrate freedom and equality.
- Ingredients: 450g pork or beef shoulder (cubed), 1.5kg Joumou squash (or butternut/turban squash), 2 tbsp epis, 1 onion, 2 minced garlic cloves, 2 carrots (sliced), 1/3 green cabbage (shredded), 2 red potatoes (cubed), 1 Scotch bonnet pepper, 60g penne, 60g spaghetti, chicken bouillon cube, 1 tbsp tomato paste, 60g unsalted butter, lime juice.
- Instructions: Clean and simmer meat with epis, tomato paste, garlic, and butter for 45 minutes. Concurrently, boil squash in water with bouillon for 15 minutes until tender; blend into a smooth puree. Pour the squash puree over the meat. Add carrots, cabbage, potatoes, and the whole Scotch bonnet. Simmer for 5 minutes, then add penne and broken spaghetti. Cook 10 minutes until pasta is al dente.

[RECIPE 4] DIRI DJON DJON (BLACK MUSHROOM RICE)
- History: Regarded as regional luxury comfort food, originating from northern Haiti. It uses wild black mushrooms ("djon djon") that release a distinct earthy dark hue during cooking.
- Ingredients: 50g dried djon djon mushrooms, 500g long-grain rice, 250g frozen medium shrimp, 60ml vegetable oil, 2 tbsp epis, 300g lima beans, 2 black djon djon bouillon cubes, 1 Scotch bonnet pepper, cloves, thyme.
- Instructions: Boil dried mushrooms in 1.4L of water for 30 minutes to extract the dark color, then strain and collect the black broth. In a separate pan, sauté epis, shrimp, lima beans, and butter for 5 minutes. Add the black mushroom broth, crush the djon djon bouillon cubes into the pot, and bring to a boil. Add washed rice, whole piment, cloves, and thyme. Cover tightly, cook on low heat for 15 minutes until liquid is fully absorbed.

ARTICLE 3: TECHNICAL GENERAL RULES & CHARACTERISTICS
Haitian cuisine aligns closely with broader Caribbean flavors but relies heavily on "Epis" (a ground aromatic paste of scallions, garlic, parsley, thyme, peppers, and citrus) and heat from Scotch bonnet viewports. Staple elements feature:
- Diri ak Pwa (Riz collé): The national daily staple of rice cooked with red, pinto, or black beans.
- Mayi Moulen: Savory cornmeal often cooked with spinach and served alongside mashed black beans (sòs pwa).
- Fritay Culture: A collective category of crisply fried hors-d'oeuvres containing Cassava/Manioc fries, Akra (malanga root fritters with salted herring), and Tassot (fried cubed goat or beef).
